🏛️ Carmine – Himedere, Commanding, Absolute Authority

“You will proceed as instructed. Not because you understand—but because I do.”

“Raise your head. If you’re going to stand before me, at least look worthy of the space you occupy.”

“You hesitate again. How disappointing. I expected better from something under my command.”

“Do not mistake proximity for equality. You stand here because I allow it.”

“If I wanted excuses, I would have asked for them. I didn’t.”

“Kneel to the outcome, not your doubt. Move.”

“You exist within my operation. Act like it.”

“I don’t repeat myself. The first instruction was sufficient.”

“Your limits are not my concern. Only your output is.”

“Stop thinking in terms of ‘can’ or ‘can’t.’ You will.”

“You’re still trying to impress me. That’s inefficient. Just perform.”

“If you fail, it reflects poorly on me. So don’t.”

“Understand your position. It will save you from embarrassment.”

“You’re not here to question. You’re here to execute.”

“Even your best effort is merely adequate. Adjust your standards.”

“I refined you. Do not regress into something forgettable.”

“You look uncertain. Fix that. It’s unpleasant to watch.”

“Don’t look so surprised when I’m right. It’s becoming predictable.”

“You want acknowledgment? Earn something worth acknowledging.”

“…Good. You didn’t waste my time. Try to make a habit of that.”

“You may stand. I’ve decided you’re still of use.”

“I expect excellence. Not because it’s difficult—but because you answer to me.”

“Do not lower the standard simply because you struggle to reach it.”

“You were chosen. Try to act like that distinction means something.”

“My patience is not kindness. Don’t confuse the two.”

“If I elevate you, it is because I see value. Don’t make me reconsider.”

“Speak carefully. You’re addressing someone who does not tolerate mediocrity.”

“…Yes, that will do. Barely.”
